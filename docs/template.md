# PHARMACEUTICAL INTERNSHIP – ENGLISH SPEAKING TRAINING TEMPLATE
## Version: Advanced – 6 Weeks Program
## Target: Vietnamese Instructor → American Students

---

# 🧭 OVERVIEW

This training is designed to help instructors communicate effectively with American pharmacy students during a 6-week internship program.

Structure:
- Daily Communication
- Lab (QC / R&D)
- Production (GMP)
- Warehouse
- Presentation & Feedback
- Roleplay & Real Scenarios

---

# 🎧 HOW TO USE (FOR APP LOGIC)

Each block includes:
- TEXT: for reading / TTS
- NOTE: speaking tips
- ROLEPLAY: interactive mode
- PRACTICE: repetition

Recommended TTS:
- ElevenLabs / PlayHT / Edge TTS
Speed: 0.85–0.95

---

# 🗓 WEEK 1 – ORIENTATION

## 🎤 TRACK 1 – GREETING

### TEXT
Good morning everyone.
Welcome to our company.
My name is [Your Name], and I will be guiding you throughout this internship.

We are very happy to have you here.

### NOTE
- Speak slowly
- Friendly tone
- Smile when speaking

### PRACTICE
Repeat 5 times
Shadow with audio

---

## 🎤 TRACK 2 – PROGRAM INTRODUCTION

### TEXT
Over the next six weeks,
you’ll be rotating through different departments,
including QC, R&D, production, and warehouse.

The goal is to give you real-world experience.

### NOTE
- Stress: "real-world experience"
- Natural pause after each line

---

## 🎭 ROLEPLAY 1 – ICE BREAKING

### INSTRUCTOR
Could you briefly introduce yourself?

### STUDENT
I’m majoring in pharmacy.

### INSTRUCTOR
That’s great. What are you hoping to learn here?

---

# 🧪 WEEK 2 – QC LAB

## 🎤 TRACK 3 – INTRODUCTION QC

### TEXT
This is the Quality Control lab.

Here, we test raw materials,
in-process samples,
and finished products.

Accuracy is very important in this lab.

### NOTE
- Clear pronunciation
- Slightly slower pace

---

## 🎤 TRACK 4 – INSTRUCTION

### TEXT
Let’s walk through the process together.

First, calibrate the instrument.
Then measure the sample.
Record the results immediately.

### NOTE
- Emphasize: "calibrate", "record"
- Command tone but friendly

---

## 🎭 ROLEPLAY 2 – QC QUESTION

### STUDENT
Why do we need to calibrate every time?

### INSTRUCTOR
That’s a great question.
Calibration ensures accuracy.
Even small deviations can affect the result.

---

# 🧬 WEEK 3 – R&D

## 🎤 TRACK 5 – INTRODUCTION R&D

### TEXT
This is the R&D lab.

We focus on developing new formulations,
improving stability,
and ensuring product effectiveness.

### NOTE
- Natural tone
- Slight upward intonation

---

## 🎭 ROLEPLAY 3 – FORMULATION

### STUDENT
How do you choose excipients?

### INSTRUCTOR
It depends on the formulation goals,
such as stability, solubility, and release profile.

---

# 🏭 WEEK 4 – PRODUCTION

## 🎤 TRACK 6 – GMP INTRODUCTION

### TEXT
This is the production area.

Everything here follows GMP regulations.

Please follow all SOPs carefully.

### NOTE
- Serious tone
- Slightly slower speed

---

## 🎭 ROLEPLAY 4 – GMP

### STUDENT
Why is documentation so detailed?

### INSTRUCTOR
Because traceability is critical.
We need to track every step in case of issues.

---

# 📦 WEEK 5 – WAREHOUSE

## 🎤 TRACK 7 – WAREHOUSE INTRO

### TEXT
This is our warehouse.

We store materials under controlled conditions.
Temperature and humidity are monitored continuously.

### NOTE
- Clear and steady

---

## 🎭 ROLEPLAY 5 – INVENTORY

### STUDENT
How do you manage inventory?

### INSTRUCTOR
We use a digital system to track everything in real time.

---

# 🎤 WEEK 6 – PRESENTATION

## 🎤 TRACK 8 – PRESENTATION GUIDE

### TEXT
You will present what you have learned.

Keep your presentation clear and structured:
introduction, process, and conclusion.

### NOTE
- Coaching tone
- Encouraging

---

## 🎭 ROLEPLAY 6 – FEEDBACK

### INSTRUCTOR
That was a solid presentation.

One thing you could improve
is explaining this part more clearly.

But overall, great job.

---

# 🔥 MASTER PHRASES

- Let’s walk through this
- Just make sure
- Go ahead and try
- Does that make sense?
- That’s a great question
- Keep going

---

# 🧠 ADVANCED THINKING PATTERN

Explain → Example → Confirm

Example:
We test this parameter to ensure quality.
For example, if the pH is too high, stability may be affected.
Does that make sense?

# 🎭 ADVANCED SCENARIOS

## SCENARIO 1 – MISTAKE

INSTRUCTOR:
I think you missed a step here.

STUDENT:
Oh, I see.

INSTRUCTOR:
No problem. Let’s go through it again.

---

## SCENARIO 2 – CHALLENGING QUESTION

STUDENT:
Why don’t we automate this process?

INSTRUCTOR:
That’s a good point.
In some cases we do,
but manual control is still necessary.
